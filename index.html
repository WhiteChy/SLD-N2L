SLD-N2L: A Speaker-Lombard-Disentangled Normal-to-Lombard Conversion

Abstract

The Normal-to-Lombard (N2L) conversion can effectively enhance speech intelligibility in noisy communication scenarios and serve as a data augmentation tool for various speech-related algorithms. However, current N2L methods typically rely solely on noise levels as the guiding label for Lombard style conversion and use general loss functions to approximate generated features to those of Lombard speech. In this paper, we propose a Speaker-Lombard-Disentangled N2L conversion model (SLD-N2L). We disentangle the speaker and Lombard effect by fine-tuning the speaker encoder. Then, we fuse Lombard embedding and speaker embedding to obtain speaker-aware Lombard embedding for accurate conversion. We also propose the Lombard loss that uses a parameter predictor to close the generated waveform and ground-truth waveform in Lombard-related parameters. Experimental results demonstrate that our proposed method significantly outperforms existing N2L models. Ablation study shows that proposed speaker-aware Lombard embedding and Lombard loss can improve both speech quality and intelligibility.
